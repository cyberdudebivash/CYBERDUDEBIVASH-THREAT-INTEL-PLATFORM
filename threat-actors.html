Frequently Asked Questions

What is an APT group?
An Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group is a sophisticated, typically nation-state-sponsored threat actor that conducts sustained, targeted cyberattack campaigns against specific organizations or sectors. APT groups use advanced techniques, maintain long-term access to compromised networks, and are motivated by espionage, sabotage, or financial gain. SENTINEL APEX tracks 40+ confirmed APT groups.
How does SENTINEL APEX attribute threat actors?
SENTINEL APEX uses multi-source attribution including TTP correlation with MITRE ATT&CK framework, malware code similarity analysis, infrastructure reuse patterns, victimology overlap, operational timing analysis, and corroboration with government advisories (CISA, NCSC, FBI). Attribution is always expressed with a confidence level and does not go beyond what evidence supports.
How can threat actor intelligence help my SOC?
Threat actor intelligence enables proactive defense by helping SOC teams understand who is likely to target them, what TTPs those actors use, what vulnerabilities they exploit, and what their infrastructure looks like. This allows detection rules to be tuned for specific actor behaviors, threat hunts to focus on relevant IOCs, and vulnerability patching to prioritize CVEs actors in your sector are actively exploiting.
