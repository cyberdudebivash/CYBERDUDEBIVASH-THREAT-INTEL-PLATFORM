Frequently Asked Questions — IOC Intelligence

What are Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)?
Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) are forensic artifacts that indicate a network, system, or device has been breached. They include malicious IP addresses, domains, file hashes, URLs, and email addresses associated with threat actors, malware, and attack campaigns. SOC teams use IOCs to detect, investigate, and respond to cyber incidents.
How does SENTINEL APEX aggregate IOC feeds?
SENTINEL APEX ingests 74+ threat intelligence feeds including commercial, open-source, government, and community sources. All feeds are ingested every 6 hours, deduplicated, enriched with contextual metadata, correlated with campaign and actor intelligence, and exported in STIX 2.1 and MISP formats for integration with SIEM, SOAR, and EDR platforms.
Can I integrate IOC feeds with my SIEM?
Yes. SENTINEL APEX provides REST API endpoints delivering IOC data in JSON, CSV, STIX 2.1, and MISP formats, compatible with Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, IBM QRadar, Elastic SIEM, and all major SIEM platforms. TAXII 2.1 endpoints are also available for standards-compliant automated feed ingestion.
