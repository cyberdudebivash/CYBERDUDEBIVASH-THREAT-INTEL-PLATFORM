# 1. Purpose

This document defines the **Threat Intelligence Model** used by the CyberDudeBivash Sentinel APEX platform.

The model describes how the platform:

• collects threat signals  
• normalizes intelligence  
• correlates events  
• predicts emerging threats  
• detects zero-day activity  
• calculates the Global Threat Index (GTI)

The objective is to transform fragmented security signals into **actionable intelligence**.

---

# 2. Threat Intelligence Philosophy

Sentinel APEX operates on the principle that:

**Threat intelligence emerges from signal convergence.**

Single events rarely indicate a threat.  
However, when multiple signals align, they reveal patterns such as:

• exploitation campaigns  
• emerging vulnerabilities  
• attacker infrastructure  
• coordinated attack waves  

The Sentinel APEX intelligence model focuses on **correlating weak signals into strong intelligence**.

---

# 3. Intelligence Signal Types

Sentinel APEX processes multiple signal categories.

### Vulnerability Signals

Examples:

• CVE disclosures  
• security advisories  
• vulnerability research publications  

These signals represent **potential attack surfaces**.

---

### Exploit Signals

Examples:

• exploit proof-of-concepts  
• exploit repository commits  
• exploit toolkit updates  

These signals indicate **weaponization of vulnerabilities**.

---

### Threat Activity Signals

Examples:

• attacker infrastructure discovery  
• malware campaigns  
• scanning activity spikes  

These signals represent **active threat actor behavior**.

---

### Intelligence Feed Signals

External threat feeds provide contextual information such as:

• indicators of compromise (IOCs)  
• threat actor activity  
• infrastructure indicators  

These feeds enrich the intelligence graph.
