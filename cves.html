Frequently Asked Questions — CVE Intelligence

What is EPSS scoring for CVEs?
EPSS (Exploit Prediction Scoring System) is a probability score (0–1) indicating the likelihood a CVE will be exploited in the wild within 30 days. SENTINEL APEX provides real-time EPSS scores for all CVEs to help security teams prioritize patching beyond CVSS severity alone. A CVE with CVSS 9.8 and EPSS 0.01 may be lower priority than a CVE with CVSS 7.0 and EPSS 0.85.
What is the difference between CVE and CISA KEV?
CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) is the global catalog of known vulnerabilities maintained by MITRE. CISA KEV (Known Exploited Vulnerabilities) is a curated subset maintained by CISA listing vulnerabilities confirmed to be actively exploited in real-world attacks. KEV vulnerabilities require immediate remediation under CISA's Binding Operational Directive 22-01 for US federal agencies — and are high-priority for all enterprises.
How does SENTINEL APEX prioritize CVEs?
SENTINEL APEX combines CVSS base score, EPSS exploit prediction, CISA KEV status, active exploitation evidence, threat actor weaponization signals, and asset exposure context to generate a composite priority score. This enables security teams to focus patching resources where actual risk is highest, not just where CVSS scores are highest.
How often is CVE data updated?
CVE data is ingested from NIST NVD and CISA KEV continuously, with a maximum update latency of 15 minutes. EPSS scores are refreshed daily. Exploitation signals from threat feeds and honeypots are processed in near-real-time.
