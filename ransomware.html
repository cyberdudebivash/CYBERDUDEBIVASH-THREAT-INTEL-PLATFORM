Frequently Asked Questions

What is Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)?
Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) is a cybercrime business model where ransomware operators lease their malware and infrastructure to affiliates who conduct the actual attacks, sharing ransom proceeds. The operator maintains the ransomware code, C2 infrastructure, and victim payment portal, while affiliates handle initial access, lateral movement, and deployment. Most major ransomware groups (LockBit, BlackCat/ALPHV, etc.) operate as RaaS.
How does SENTINEL APEX monitor ransomware victim sites?
SENTINEL APEX monitors ransomware group Tor-based leak sites, Telegram channels, and dark web forums for victim publication announcements. When a new victim is published by a ransomware group, SENTINEL APEX processes the data within hours, providing early warning to organizations in targeted sectors and geographies, even before a victim has publicly disclosed an incident.
What CVEs do ransomware groups commonly exploit?
Ransomware groups frequently exploit vulnerabilities in VPNs (Fortinet, Cisco, Ivanti), RDP (Windows), Exchange servers, and internet-facing systems. SENTINEL APEX tracks which CVEs each group actively weaponizes and correlates this with CISA KEV data to provide combined prioritization signals. Virtually all major ransomware groups exploit vulnerabilities in the CISA KEV catalog.
