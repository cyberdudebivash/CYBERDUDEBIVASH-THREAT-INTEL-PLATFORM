Our Editorial Mission

CYBERDUDEBIVASH SENTINEL APEX publishes threat intelligence with the singular mission of helping security teams detect, respond to, and prevent cyberattacks. Every piece of intelligence we publish is held to the standards below. We do not publish intelligence for sensationalism, commercial advantage at the expense of accuracy, or attribution without evidence.

Our editorial standards are based on the FIRST TLP (Traffic Light Protocol) framework, responsible disclosure principles, and established threat intelligence community norms.

Accuracy Standards

Minimum Source Requirement

All intelligence published on SENTINEL APEX is verified against a minimum of two independent, authoritative sources before publication. For attribution claims and nation-state actor profiling, we require corroboration from at least three sources including at least one government advisory or peer-reviewed security research publication.

Confidence Levels

All published intelligence is tagged with an explicit confidence level:

  • HIGH — Multiple independent sources confirm. Technical evidence corroborated.
  • MEDIUM — Single strong source or multiple lower-confidence sources. Plausible but not definitive.
  • LOW — Preliminary intelligence, early indicators, or single unverified source. Treat as hypothesis.

Corrections Policy

When errors are identified in published intelligence, we issue a correction within 24 hours, clearly noting the original content and what was corrected. Corrections are not deleted — they are appended to the original record for full auditability.

TLP Classification Framework

All intelligence published on SENTINEL APEX uses the FIRST Traffic Light Protocol (TLP) classification system:

TLP:CLEAR

No restrictions. May be shared freely and distributed publicly.

TLP:GREEN

Community sharing. May be shared within the security community but not publicly posted.

TLP:AMBER

Limited sharing. Restricted to organization and partners with need-to-know.

TLP:RED

Strictly confidential. Not for disclosure outside the original recipient group.

All publicly available intelligence on this platform is classified TLP:WHITE (equivalent to TLP:CLEAR under TLP 2.0). Content behind authentication may be classified TLP:GREEN or TLP:AMBER for licensed subscribers.

Responsible Disclosure

Zero-Day & Unpatched Vulnerabilities

SENTINEL APEX follows coordinated vulnerability disclosure principles. When we identify unpatched vulnerabilities through original research:

  • We notify the affected vendor privately with full technical details
  • We allow a minimum 90-day remediation window (extendable by agreement)
  • We publish a public advisory only after a patch is available or the 90-day window expires
  • We coordinate with CISA and relevant CERTs for critical infrastructure vulnerabilities
  • We may publish limited technical details earlier if exploitation is detected in the wild

Active Exploitation Intelligence

When we detect active exploitation of a vulnerability that has not yet received a patch, we balance the need for defender awareness against the risk of facilitating further exploitation. We publish IOCs, detection rules, and mitigation guidance without technical exploitation details until a patch is available.

Attribution Standards

Threat actor attribution is one of the most technically and ethically challenging aspects of threat intelligence. Our attribution standards:

  • Attribution claims are always accompanied by the evidence basis and confidence level
  • We do not publish nation-state attribution solely based on language artifacts or timezone analysis
  • We distinguish between technical attribution (infrastructure, malware code overlap) and geopolitical attribution (nation-state direction)
  • We use established threat actor naming conventions and reference aliases from multiple intelligence vendors
  • We note when our attribution differs from other credible sources
  • Premature or inaccurate attribution can cause geopolitical harm — we err on the side of caution

Data Sources

SENTINEL APEX aggregates intelligence from the following categories of sources:

  • Government advisories — CISA, NCSC-UK, BSI, ASD ACSC, FBI, ANSSI
  • Standards bodies — NIST NVD, MITRE ATT&CK, FIRST EPSS
  • Commercial threat intelligence feeds (licensed)
  • Open-source intelligence (OSINT) aggregation
  • SENTINEL APEX original research and honeypot networks
  • Community threat intelligence sharing (ISACs, MISP communities)
  • Dark web and underground forum monitoring

We do not publish intelligence obtained through unauthorized access to systems or data.
