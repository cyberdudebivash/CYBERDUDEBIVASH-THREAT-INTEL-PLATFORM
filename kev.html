Frequently Asked Questions — CISA KEV

What is the CISA KEV catalog?
The CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog is a curated list maintained by the US Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) of vulnerabilities confirmed to be actively exploited in real-world cyberattacks. US federal agencies must remediate KEV entries by specified due dates under CISA Binding Operational Directive 22-01. Enterprises globally treat KEV entries as their highest-priority vulnerabilities.
What is CISA BOD 22-01?
CISA Binding Operational Directive 22-01 mandates that all US Federal Civilian Executive Branch (FCEB) agencies must remediate vulnerabilities in the CISA KEV catalog by specified due dates. Due dates are typically 2 weeks for most entries. SENTINEL APEX tracks these due dates and provides compliance reporting to support BOD 22-01 adherence.
How often is the CISA KEV catalog updated?
CISA updates the KEV catalog multiple times per week as new actively exploited vulnerabilities are confirmed. SENTINEL APEX syncs within 15 minutes of each CISA update, immediately alerting subscribers to new KEV additions with full enrichment context including EPSS scores, affected products, and exploitation evidence.
Why should enterprises prioritize KEV beyond CVSS scores?
CVSS scores measure theoretical severity, not actual exploitation risk. Many KEV entries have moderate CVSS scores but are being actively exploited by ransomware groups and nation-state actors. The fact that a vulnerability is in the KEV catalog is the single strongest signal for immediate remediation, regardless of its CVSS score. SENTINEL APEX combines KEV status with EPSS and exploitation signals for comprehensive prioritization.
